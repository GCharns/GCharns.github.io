Student Action for Refugees

Graphic Design

STAR - Student Action for Refugees, a charity group aiming to help refugees establish themselves and their families in Sheffield, asked me to create a set of marketing assets for their music event Refugee Rhythms at Yellow Arch Studios. The brief was to create a design that stood out among other flyers and posters, and refected the positive and fun atmosphere of the night.

...

Refugee Rhythms Event #2

For the second event I worked with Ishita Gupta, another volunteer at STAR, to create the designs. Held in December in the wintery cold of the north, we wanted to juxtapose the bleak conditions by promising a warm evening that had real energy. We decided on a hot red and yellow colour scheme and Ishita provided some fantastic sketches to work with.

The scanned drawings acted as a good starting point as they gave me some elements to play with. I really enjoyed cutting out the simpler elements and repeating them in different patterns for different effects. I decided to compliment the hand drawn texture with a subtly textured background and blended the sketches into the design using layer effects.

...

Refugee Rhythms Event #3

The poster for this event went through many iterations. Ishita kindly helped out again with drawing assets, which gave us a nice toolset to begin playing with. The theme for this event, to be held in March, was Spring.

These drawings provided an invaluable starting point for the design of the posters. I tried putting them together in various combinations but found them difficult to place together, settling on a leaf print background and a mirror of one of the more abstract flower drawings. A vibrant green colour scheme was chosen to reflect the freshness of spring.

To conclude, the real value behind this design work was not in the artifacts produced, but in the collaborative process and the resulting event. It was my first experience working with others on design work, which I really believe enriched the end product, and the events themselves showed great support for local refugees, who were in attendance and gave thanks for the support provided by all in attendance.
